Machine learning is a branch of artificial intelligence focused on building systems that learn patterns from data without explicit programming for every task. Deep learning uses layered neural networks to process complex data like text, images, and audio. Transformers revolutionized natural language processing by introducing self-attention mechanisms that capture long-range dependencies across sequences of words.
Long Filter Mixer (LFM) replaces self-attention with hierarchical linear-time filters to handle ultra-long sequences efficiently. Unlike transformers with O(L²) complexity, LFM runs in O(L) time and memory, making it suitable for documents with tens of thousands of tokens.
Causal language modeling requires the model to predict the next token autoregressively, only accessing historical context without future information. The causal filter design in LFM strictly prevents information leakage from later positions in the sequence.
SwiGLU feed-forward layers improve modeling capacity compared to standard ReLU, widely adopted in modern sequence architectures including Hyena, Mamba and LFM. Layer normalization before each sub-module stabilizes training for deep stacked layers.
